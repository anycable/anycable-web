The landscape

Three ways to run a WebSocket tier

Every realtime stack picks one of three topologies. It is the choice that drives the numbers on the pages above: what survives a deploy, how far one box scales, and who carries the cost.

In-process app server

The WebSocket lives inside your app process.

Action Cable, Solid Cable, AsyncCable, Socket.io, Reverb

Simplest to run, nothing extra to deploy. Connections drop on every app deploy, and capacity is capped by the app server well before the box is full.

Out-of-process gateway You are here

A dedicated server holds the sockets; your app handles auth and commands.

AnyCable

Survives app deploys, adds delivery guarantees and presence, and scales to hundreds of thousands of connections on one box. You run one more service.

Managed SaaS

A vendor runs the WebSocket tier, billed per connection and message.

Pusher, Ably, Liveblocks, PubNub

No infrastructure to run. Cost scales with traffic, and your realtime data flows through a third party.

AnyCable is open source and MIT-licensed. The comparisons above pit it against the in-process and managed options on the metrics that matter in production.
